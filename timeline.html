Collapse & Resistance Timeline

2040s–2050s

The Heroic Period

State and corporate-backed heroes publicly tackled crime, pollution, and dissent. Surveillance, while initially welcomed, became normalized.

2055

The Great Bangladesh Flood

Mass climate migration intensified. India’s coastal evacuation policies led to heavily militarized relief zones. Some early resistors disappear.

2060

The Disclosures

Whistleblowers inside the Yamuna Monitoring Division released documents that revealed dangerous coverups of floodplain construction.

2061

Collapse of Trust

The AI-generated National Curriculum was introduced. History was revised; unauthorized records of the Resistance were deleted.

2065

Resistance Formation

Clusters of oral historians, students, archivists, and rogue librarians began training others in salt lamp recovery.

2069

The Yamuna Breach

Heavy rainfall combined with blocked drainage led to the collapse of four embankments. Thousands were displaced. Delhi drowned.

2072

Rohan Kapoor Disappears

During a raid on the Archive Vault, Rohan vanishes. His last known location was near the submerged Satcomm tower.

2075

Sumati Activates the Lamp

A young girl uncovers an old Salt Lamp. Stories whisper back. The Firebird returns. The Archive begins to pulse again.
